One app, gently divided

Everything, folded into one calm app.

01 - 07 Loose & light

Tasks

Capture errands, client notes, and small intentions without forcing each into a calendar slot. Flip between your inbox and just today, group everything into lists, and when something needs more, a task quietly holds it: a checklist, a reminder, the link or file you'll want when you get to it.

Toggle Inbox / Today, swipe for all lists and an open task
02 - 07 Timed blocks

Calendar

Scheduled time in soft blocks: enough structure to read the day, enough air to breathe. See it as a list, a single day, two or three days side by side, the full week as a grid or a compact agenda, the classic month, or the whole year at a glance. Color each day by role or by list to see the shape of your time. Your Apple and Google calendars flow in too, so nothing lives in two places.

Swipe the phone: List, Day, Multiday, Week, Month, Year
03 - 07 Move things across

Projects

Wrap any project, whether a trip, a launch, a home move, or a client job, into a board and slide cards across it as things progress. A card can hold cover photos and links, start and due dates, a file, or a checklist inside, and turn into a task the moment it needs doing.

Swipe: Boards, Cards, Dates, Checklists, Detail, Actions
04 - 07 Quiet rhythms

Habits

Repeated rhythms, tracked gently. Give one a goal if you want, ongoing or a run of days, and how often it should happen, down to a few times a day. Then watch the month, or the whole year, quietly fill in. No streak to defend.

Swipe: Habits, Detail, Overall, Gallery, New
05 - 07 A line a day

Journal

Keep reflections close to the day, so the timeline carries emotional context, not only events. Keep separate spaces for whatever you write: a diary, quick notes, Sunday's sermon, a place for ideas, so it doesn't all pile into one list.

Swipe: Diary, Spaces
06 - 07 People, not reminders

Birthdays

The people you care about surface right inside Today, so a birthday never quietly slips past you again. Add a small note too: gift ideas, or the flowers they love. Set a gentle nudge a week ahead, the day before, or the morning of, enough time to actually do something about it.

07 - 07 Gentle focus

Pomodoro

Focus when you want it: rounds, short breaks, and a quiet ring. A calm timer, never pressure to perform.

Routines, woven in

Your routines live in the day, not in your head.

Most productivity apps quietly skip the things you do every day. So your morning, the school run, a coding block end up nowhere, or get re-added by hand. Dayfold keeps recurring routines right in the calendar, where the day actually happens.

And when you'd rather see only what's new, hide them with one tap. For anyone whose day runs on structure, that small switch is the whole point.

What you actually get back

Your day, told by role, not by a to-do count.

Most apps end the day with “3 of 7 done.” Dayfold can end it with a softer, more honest picture: which aspects of life took your hours, in clear percentages, and where the gaps are.

Why it helps: over a week the pattern speaks for itself. The role that quietly slipped away, the one that quietly took everything. Not to optimize, just to notice, so you can rebalance on purpose instead of by accident. And if it’s not for you, switch roles off entirely.

Why Dayfold exists

Dayfold is made by one person: a tattoo artist, a mother, and a developer who needed a single calm place to hold all of it. It’s shaped every day around real days: client sessions, school runs, code, and the quiet hours in between.

FAQ

For people who want to feel present again.

Is Dayfold a productivity app?

Not in the usual sense. Dayfold includes tasks and schedules, but the point is not to squeeze more out of the day. It helps you see where time, attention, roles, and energy actually went.

Does it punish unfinished days?

No. Dayfold avoids guilt mechanics. Unfinished days still tell you something. The point is visibility, not another scoreboard.

Does it work with my existing calendar?

Yes. Dayfold reads your Apple and Google calendars, so the events you already keep show up right alongside your habits, tasks, and roles. No copying, no second place to check.

Who can see what I write?

Only you. Your days, journal entries, and notes stay private by default. Dayfold is a mirror you glance at and close, not a feed for anyone else.

Will it stay free?

Dayfold is free while in beta. After launch there will be one simple, fairly priced plan. Never ads, and never your data as the price. Beta testers will hear about it first.

When is the Mac app coming?

Dayfold lives on iPhone first. The Mac app is next in line and already in the works. Join the beta and you’ll know the moment it lands.
